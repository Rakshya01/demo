A.P.J Abdul Kalam

1931-2015

Missile Man of India

Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, full name Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, was an eminent Indian aerospace scientist and the 11th President of India (2002-2007). He is widely regarded as one of the most inspiring leaders in India’s modern history and is often referred to as the "Missile Man of India" for his significant contributions to the country’s missile development programs. He was also loved by many as the "People's President" due to his humble and approachable nature.Dr. Abdul Kalam’s legacy as a scientist, educator, and leader continues to inspire millions of people, particularly the youth of India. His life is a testament to the power of perseverance, simplicity, and vision.

Biographies

  • Studied physics at St. Joseph’s College, Tiruchirappalli.
  • Later pursued aerospace engineering at the Madras Institute of Technology (MIT).
  • Worked as a project director for India's first satellite launch vehicle (SLV-III).
  • Successfully deployed the Rohini satellite into space in 1980.
  • Key figure in the development of India’s missile systems, including Agni and Prithvi missiles.
